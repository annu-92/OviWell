Scientific Facts About Menstruation

Menstruation is a natural physiological process that involves the shedding of the uterine lining. The menstrual fluid contains:

  • Fresh blood (50-75%)
  • Cervical and vaginal secretions
  • Endometrial tissue

The pH level of menstrual blood is between 6.5-8.0, which is not conducive to bacterial growth. The body has natural mechanisms to maintain hygiene during menstruation.


Exercise During Menstruation: The Science

Scientific research shows that exercise during menstruation can:

  • Release endorphins that act as natural painkillers
  • Improve blood circulation, reducing cramping
  • Help reduce prostaglandins, hormones responsible for period pain
  • Boost serotonin levels, improving mood

Studies indicate that moderate exercise can reduce PMS symptoms by up to 30%.


Understanding Irregular Periods

Scientific research shows that irregular periods can be caused by various factors:

  • Hormonal imbalances
  • Stress and lifestyle factors
  • Thyroid disorders
  • Polycystic Ovary Syndrome (PCOS)

Many of these conditions are treatable, and having irregular periods doesn't automatically indicate fertility issues.
